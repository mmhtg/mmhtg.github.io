Men's Mental Health Talking Group in Cornwall, UK

What

Men's mental health talking support group

We are a supportive group of men sharing real-life experience, advice & support in a safe location. We run informal, round table support group talking sessions guided by trained male facilitators.

Confidential & non-judgemental. Free to attend. Never any pressure to talk.

If & when you choose to talk, we will listen & you will be heard.

No booking or referral, just turn up. Tea, coffee, water & biscuits provided.

How Meetings Work

[Work in progress.]

Your First Meeting

You are welcome to join a full (2 hour) meeting, or either half (1 hour) if that feels more appropriate.

You can talk (share) with the the group, or you can listen. You are never pressured or expected to talk. It is common for new attendees to take time settling in before sharing, while others need to talk openly in their first meeting. You choose your route in. It's a meeting run for you, and you choose the level of your involvement.

Please arrive at the venue before 7pm or during the halftime break from 7:55pm – 8:10pm, if you can. This helps us welcome you properly with minimal disruption to other group members who are sharing during the talking meeting.

Support & Donations

Group meetings are free to join & attend, as often or infrequently as you choose. There is no entrance charge or membership fee to pay. There is no product or service to buy. Meetings are funded primarily by donations from our well-wishing supporters, and fundraising & outreach activities.

Group attendees are never asked for donations. A donation collection pot is located discreetly at the venue away from the meeting should an attendee choose to donate to their local group. Group donations are kept locally & used towards funding the running costs of the meeting, including venue hire, refreshments, liability insurance etc.
